# Product

## Register

product

## Users

People who split shared expenses in groups — roommates managing household costs, friends on trips, project teams sharing supplies, event organizers pooling funds. They use EVM wallets (no email/password), want real-time visibility into who owes what, and need an easy path from tracking to settlement.

## Product Purpose

Make splitting expenses effortless and transparent. StableSplit replaces messy IOUs and manual tracking with organized groups, auto-calculated balances, and optional on-chain settlement. Success means users never wonder "who paid last time" or "do I owe them money."

## Brand Personality

Warm, Clear, Confident. The app should feel friendly and approachable (like Monzo or Cash App) — not cold or corporate. Finances are stressful; the interface should reduce anxiety, not add to it. Tone is helpful and human, never robotic.

## Anti-references

No specific anti-references. General direction: avoid generic fintech clichés, dark-mode-by-default, and Splitwise-clone visual language.

## Design Principles

1. **Reduce friction, not complexity** — Every screen should answer "what do I owe?" within one glance. Group finances are inherently complex; the UI's job is to surface the answer, not hide the details.
2. **Trust through transparency** — Balances, settlements, and activity logs show exactly what happened, when, and why. No magic numbers. Each calculation is explainable.
3. **Warm competence** — The interface is friendly and approachable without sacrificing precision. Money matters demand accuracy; warmth makes the experience human.
4. **Inclusive by default** — WCAG AA compliance, readable text, keyboard-navigable, reduced motion support. Accessibility is not a feature; it's the baseline.
5. **Web-native, wallet-first** — No email/password. The wallet IS the identity. Design for the connected wallet experience — account switching, chain awareness, transaction feedback.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA target. Keyboard-navigable, proper focus indicators, semantic HTML, contrast ≥4.5:1 for body text. Reduced motion support for all animations.
